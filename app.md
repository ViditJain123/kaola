## Name

Lockin Club

## One-line pitch

A productivity app that locks every app on your phone until you actually grind — record timelapses of your work to earn screen time back.

## The problem it solves

Students and young professionals know they should be locked in, but their phone wins every time. They open it to "check one thing" and lose two hours to TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube. They want proof they're working hard — both for themselves and to show the world — but most productivity apps are silent, boring, and easy to ignore. Discipline alone isn't working; they need a system that makes phones stop being the enemy and turns work into something you can actually flex.

## All features

1. Sleep-time auto-lock — in onboarding you set your sleep time, and at that exact hour every app on your phone goes locked.
2. Grind to unlock — to use any locked app you have to start a grind session. Two modes: timelapse (records your work, saves a video) or non-timelapse (just tracks the time).
3. Strava-style timelapse intros — every timelapse opens with a 2-second stats frame (duration, date, totals) before cutting into the sped-up footage with a live timer overlay. Designed to be shareable.
4. Fixed earn rate — every 1 hour of grind = 15 minutes of screen time. Simple, predictable, no gimmicks.
5. Daily lock-in goal — pick a target in onboarding (e.g. 6 hours). Hit it, and every app unlocks freely until your sleep time.
6. Streaks, analytics, and a global monthly leaderboard so the grind compounds and competes.
7. Profile + one-tap share to social — post your timelapses straight to Instagram, TikTok, or stories to flex the work.

## Target audience

Students and working professionals in their early 20s (roughly 19–26), globally but skewing toward markets where "lock in" / "study with me" / grind-culture content already dominates feeds (US, UK, India, SEA). They already follow productivity creators, watch StudyTok and "day in my life" timelapses, and like the *idea* of being disciplined more than they currently are. They want a visible identity around hard work — not just the work itself.

## What NOT to say

- No shame-based messaging ("you're addicted to your phone," "you're wasting your life") — we're a tool for people who already want to grind, not a guilt trip.
- No soft wellness language: avoid "mindful," "balance," "self-care," "journey," "habits that stick."
- Don't promise life transformation or specific outcomes ("get into Harvard," "become a millionaire," "10x your output").
- Don't name-drop competitors (Forest, Opal, Brick, Cold Turkey, One Sec, etc.) — we don't punch sideways.
- Don't call it a "screen time app" or "focus app" — it's a grind app / lock-in app.
- Avoid "AI-powered" — we're not pitching AI, we're pitching discipline with receipts.
- Don't infantilize the audience or use boomer-coded "kids these days" framing.
